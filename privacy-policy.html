Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 18, 2026. This policy explains how this keyword research website may collect, use, and protect information.

Overview

Free Keyword Search Volume Tool is built to provide estimated keyword research metrics. The current demo runs in the browser and does not require account registration.

Information We May Collect

We may collect information you voluntarily submit through a contact form, such as your name, email address, and message. If analytics or advertising tools are added, they may collect standard technical information such as browser type, device information, referring pages, and approximate location.

Keyword Searches

Keywords entered into the demo tool are processed in the browser for estimated results. If a verified keyword data API is connected later, submitted keywords may be sent to that provider to return keyword metrics.

Cookies and Advertising

This website may use cookies for analytics, security, preferences, and advertising if those services are enabled. Third-party advertising partners may use cookies to serve relevant ads according to their own privacy policies.

Cookie and Privacy Note

If Google AdSense, analytics, consent tools, or affiliate tracking are added, visitors may see cookie notices or browser-level controls depending on location and applicable privacy laws. You can manage cookies in your browser settings at any time.

How Information Is Used

  • To respond to contact requests.
  • To improve site performance, usability, and content.
  • To monitor abuse, spam, or technical problems.
  • To support analytics or advertising integrations when enabled.

Third-Party Services

The site may integrate with keyword data providers, analytics platforms, CDN services, or ad networks. These providers process information according to their own terms and privacy policies.

Third-Party Keyword API Disclaimer

Production keyword data may come from Google Ads Keyword Planner API, DataForSEO, SerpAPI, Google Trends or pytrends backends, SEMrush, Ahrefs, or similar providers. API keys should be stored in secure backend environment variables and not in public HTML, CSS, or JavaScript files.

Your Choices

You can disable cookies in your browser settings, avoid submitting personal information, or contact us to request deletion of information you provided directly.

Contact

For privacy questions, visit the Contact page.
